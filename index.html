Welcome

I am a researcher in Healthcare Operations Management at Sun Yat-Sen University . My work focuses on leveraging data-driven approaches, analytical and empirical models to optimize patient flow, physician productivity, and resource allocation in multi-channel healthcare systems.

Learn More About My Research

Research

My research interests include Healthcare Operations Management, Sustainability Operations Management, and Big Data Analytics. Below are some of my ongoing projects:

  • Healthcare Big Data Analytics: Integrated patient choice behavior data (e.g., healthcare decisions, satisfaction ratings) and physician working schedules to analyze the impact of emotional communication on patient decision-making, identifying key emotional factors and their mechanisms.
  • Sustainability Operations Management: Collaborated with schools and enterprises in impoverished areas and designed the supply chain contracts for poverty alleviation.
  • Big Data Analytic: Utilized natural language processing (NLP) techniques to perform sentiment analysis on doctor-patient communication texts, identifying emotional tendencies (e.g., positive, negative, or neutral) in dialogues.

Working Papers:

  • 1: Collaborate to Compete: Gender Differences in Physician Outpatient Behavior Post-Hierarchical Team Appointment Adoption: This study explores how hierarchical team appointments (HTA) influence physician outpatient behaviors, revealing gender differences in collaboration and competition, and the impact of team composition on consultation length, offering insights for optimizing healthcare team design and performance.
  • 2: Knowledge Transfer in Hierarchy Physician Teams: The Mediated Role of Time Pressure: The study explores the role of time pressure in mediating knowledge transfer within hierarchical physician teams, examining factors like absorptive capacity, team dynamics, and transfer barriers to understand their impact on outpatient performance.
  • 3: Do Hierarchy Physician Team Appointment Channel Balance Outpatient Service Imbalance?: This study investigates the impact of hierarchical team appointment channels (HAC) on patient flow and physician capacity utilization in outpatient services, aiming to address service imbalances and enhance operational efficiency by analyzing changes in appointment volumes and utilization before and after HAC participation.
  • 4: Research on the Adoption of Medical AI Services Based on the Health Belief Model: This study aims to explore the adoption of medical AI services by applying the Health Belief Model, leveraging insights from various large language models to understand factors influencing acceptance and implementation in healthcare settings.
